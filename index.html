Frequently Asked Questions

How much blood do YourBrand period panties actually hold?

Our period underwear holds between 1 and 4 regular tampons' worth of flow, depending on the style:

  • Thong (Light): 1 tampon - perfect for spotting or backup
  • Bikini (Moderate): 2-3 tampons - ideal for regular days
  • High-Waist (Heavy): 3-4 tampons - designed for heavy flow days
  • Overnight (Super): 4+ tampons - extra-long gusset for 10+ hours of protection

Every pair uses our patented 4-layer system: moisture-wicking cotton top, antimicrobial layer, super-absorbent core, and leak-proof barrier.

How do I wash and care for my period underwear?

Caring for your period underwear is simple and takes less than 2 minutes:

  1. Rinse immediately after wear in cold water until the water runs clear
  2. Machine wash on a gentle cycle at 30 deg C (86 deg F) with mild detergent
  3. Hang dry - never tumble dry, as heat can damage the leak-proof layer
  4. Avoid fabric softener and bleach - they reduce absorbency over time

With proper care, your period panties will last 3-5 years (150+ washes). That's 200+ disposable products saved from landfills.

Do they smell? What about on hot days?

No - our antimicrobial silver-ion treatment neutralizes odor-causing bacteria on contact. In clinical testing, YourBrand underwear showed 99.7% odor reduction compared to standard cotton underwear.

The moisture-wicking top layer pulls moisture away from your skin quickly, so you stay dry even in Houston humidity or Phoenix heat. Thousands of women in Florida, Arizona, and Texas wear them year-round with zero complaints about odor.

Can I wear them for bladder leaks or postpartum?

Yes. While designed for menstrual flow, many customers use YourBrand for light stress incontinence, postpartum bleeding, and even as backup during IVF treatments.

The super-absorbent core and leak-proof barrier handle all types of fluids. Our Overnight Briefs are especially popular with new moms - the extra-long gusset and full-back coverage handle postpartum lochia comfortably.

Note: For moderate to heavy incontinence, we recommend consulting your healthcare provider about specialized products.
